# Editorial Template — Professional English AI Architecture Substack

Normalized by scheduler supervisor at 2026-05-20T07:24:48 from existing project strategy/MVP docs.

## Target reader
Senior AI product builders, staff/principal engineers, AI PMs, and technical founders who pay for decision-grade teardown content.

## Article structure
1. **Decision memo headline** — what architecture/product decision the reader can make after reading.
2. **Why it matters now** — current market/technical trigger with concrete sources.
3. **System/product teardown** — architecture diagram, data/control flow, bottlenecks, tradeoffs.
4. **Research-to-production lessons** — what breaks outside demos; evals, cost, latency, reliability.
5. **Implementation checklist** — 5-10 concrete steps a senior builder can apply this week.
6. **Anti-slop evidence box** — links, screenshots/data, reproducible checks; no generic AI-news summary.
7. **Paid-only appendix** — templates, code snippets, decision matrix, teardown worksheet.

## Voice rules
- English, concise, evidence-driven, senior-builder tone.
- No generic AI-news roundup; every claim must support a build/buy/ship/avoid decision.
- Prefer diagrams, checklists, and teardown tables over inspirational commentary.

## Publication cadence
- Weekly free insight post (800-1200 words) to build trust.
- Biweekly paid deep dive (2500-4000 words) with templates/checklists.
